Geoprocessing Analysis

Logan Timm

Question: How much of Florida's salt marshes are conserved?

I used a file of just Florida as my basemap with layers of natural area and salt marsh area on top.


This is what the raw data looks like before any geoprocessing, for reference



I dissolved the many polygons that were present in the Florida conserved land layer so the map would look cleaner.



I then did an intersection of the two layers.


I had some issues with QGIS and when I finally got the software to work without crashing, it took almost a full 24 hours to run the intersection!


Conclusion: a vast majority of salt marshes in Florida are in conserved land, but not all.
